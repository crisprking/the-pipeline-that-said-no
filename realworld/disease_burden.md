# Real-World Disease Burden & Patient Advocacy — Four Targets

*Every epidemiological anchor below is a CrossRef-verified primary source; every organization named is a real, independently verifiable patient-advocacy body. No individual patient quotes are fabricated — where patient voice matters, it is attributed to the advocacy organizations that carry it.*

The pipeline treated these four targets identically as a computational exercise. But the diseases behind them are not abstractions, and the clinical programs that carry the leads have concrete, verifiable indications — two of which differ from the textbook biology the target names suggest.

---

## ENPP1 — from a lethal infant calcification disease to tumor immunology

**The biology.** ENPP1 hydrolyzes the STING agonist 2′3′-cGAMP. Loss-of-function ENPP1 mutations cause **Generalized Arterial Calcification of Infancy (GACI)** and are also implicated in **pseudoxanthoma elasticum (PXE)** — the genetics were established by Rutsch and colleagues (Lorenz-Depiereux et al., *Am J Hum Genet* 2010) and extended by Nitschke et al. (*Am J Hum Genet* 2012). GACI is a rare, often fatal disorder: infants develop arterial calcification and periarticular calcification, with high mortality in the first six months of life. PXE is a chronic ectopic-mineralization disorder affecting skin, retina, and vasculature, reviewed comprehensively by Germain (*Orphanet J Rare Dis* 2017).

**The clinical reality — an oncology program, not a calcification one.** The verified clinical lead, **ISM5939** (InSilico Medicine), is in a **first-in-human Phase 1a/b trial for advanced/metastatic solid tumors** (NCT06724042, sponsor InSilico Medicine Hong Kong, enrollment 159, primary completion 2029). This is the *other* face of ENPP1: by degrading extracellular cGAMP, tumors evade STING-driven immune surveillance. Carozza et al. (*Nature Cancer* 2020) showed extracellular cGAMP is a cancer-cell-produced immunotransmitter and ENPP1 is the dominant hydrolase — making ENPP1 inhibition an immuno-oncology strategy. Only **4 ENPP1-inhibitor trials** exist on ClinicalTrials.gov — an emerging, uncrowded space, exactly matching the pipeline's read of a **data-starved** target.

**Patient advocacy.** **PXE International** (founded 1995 by Sharon and Patrick Terry) is the principal patient organization for PXE, and helped drive the ABCC6/ENPP1 research agenda. **GACI Global** supports families affected by generalized arterial calcification of infancy and the ENPP1/ABCC6 spectrum. Both are real organizations carrying the voice of an ultra-rare community that standard drug economics routinely overlook.

## NLRP3 — from rare autoinflammation to obesity and cardiovascular risk

**The biology.** The NLRP3 inflammasome drives IL-1β release. Gain-of-function mutations cause **Cryopyrin-Associated Periodic Syndromes (CAPS)** — a spectrum of rare autoinflammatory diseases. Far more commonly, NLRP3-driven IL-1 signaling contributes to **gout** (crystal-induced inflammation) and to **atherosclerosis** — Grebe, Latz and colleagues (*Circ Res* 2018) detail the NLRP3/IL-1 axis in cardiovascular disease. Gout alone is a massive global burden: the Global Burden of Disease study (Vos et al., *Lancet* 2017) tracks its worldwide prevalence in the tens of millions.

**The clinical reality — a cardiometabolic program.** The verified lead, **ISM8969** (InSilico Medicine), is in a **Phase 1, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial in healthy subjects and obese adults at risk of cardiovascular disease** (NCT07581431, RECRUITING, enrollment 100, primary completion 2027). This is the only ISM8969 trial on the registry — a **first-in-class** clinical footprint, consistent with the pipeline's finding that NLRP3 was the target where its de novo designs actually cleared the conformal potency floor (14 of 24 designs above 6.0, best lower bound 6.85).

**Patient advocacy.** The **Autoinflammatory Alliance** supports patients with CAPS and related autoinflammatory diseases, and produces clinician-facing diagnostic resources. For the far larger gout population, the **Arthritis Foundation** carries patient education and advocacy.

## TYK2 — the validated, crowded, and *solved* target

**The biology.** TYK2 is a JAK-family kinase mediating IL-23/IL-12 and type-I interferon signaling. Allosteric inhibition of its regulatory JH2 pseudokinase domain gives selectivity over JAK1/2/3. The lead disease is **plaque psoriasis**, whose global epidemiology Parisi et al. (*J Invest Dermatol* 2013) systematically reviewed — a prevalence in the low single-digit percent of the population across many countries, i.e. tens of millions of patients.

**The clinical reality — approved and mature.** **Deucravacitinib (Sotyktu, BMS)** is FDA-approved (2022). The pivotal POETYK PSO Phase 3 program (e.g. NCT03624127, COMPLETED, enrollment 666; Strober et al., *J Am Acad Dermatol* 2023) established efficacy vs. placebo and apremilast. ClinicalTrials.gov returns **~100 deucravacitinib trials** — a mature, intensely developed space. The pipeline's model was *most accurate* here (CV ρ = 0.84, reproduced at 0.83 independently) precisely because the target is data-rich — and that is exactly why it offers the least novelty: the problem is already solved.

**Patient advocacy.** The **National Psoriasis Foundation** is the principal US patient-advocacy and research-funding body for psoriatic disease.

## IRAK4 — the crowded frontier where the pipeline said "no"

**The biology.** IRAK4 is a serine/threonine kinase downstream of Toll-like and IL-1 receptors, central to innate immune signaling. In hematologic malignancy, IRAK4 (with FLT3) is a driver in subsets of **acute myeloid leukemia (AML)** and **myelodysplastic syndromes (MDS)**. AML is an aggressive disease of older adults with poor long-term survival (Shallis et al., *Blood Reviews* 2019); MDS epidemiology was quantified by Rollison et al. (*Blood* 2008). Both remain areas of high unmet need.

**The clinical reality — a crowded field with a stumble.** The lead, **emavusertib (CA-4948, Curis/Aurigene)**, appears in **10 trials**. The IRAK4/FLT3 mechanism and clinical program are reviewed by Parrondo et al. (*Front Immunol* 2023). Critically, the pivotal AML/MDS study **NCT04278768 (enrollment 366) is currently SUSPENDED**, while combination trials such as NCT03328078 (relapsed/refractory hematologic malignancies and primary CNS lymphoma) continue to recruit. This is the real-world signature of a **saturated, high-risk competitive space** — precisely the condition under which the pipeline found **zero designs that were simultaneously buildable and ownable** (0 of 36; every in-domain molecule collided with existing IP, every novel molecule sat at the edge of the applicability domain). The pipeline's "no" and the clinical field's crowding are two views of the same fact.

**Patient advocacy.** The **Leukemia & Lymphoma Society (LLS)** is the largest voluntary health organization dedicated to blood cancers, funding research and supporting AML/MDS patients. The **MDS Foundation** serves the myelodysplastic-syndrome community specifically.

## Why this layer matters for the thesis

The pipeline never saw a disease. It saw data density, applicability domains, and IP crowding. Yet its four verdicts line up, target for target, with the real-world clinical landscape:

| Target | Pipeline read | Real-world signature |
|---|---|---|
| ENPP1 | data-starved → "not yet" | 4 trials, emerging IO mechanism, ultra-rare genetics origin |
| NLRP3 | designs cleared the floor → "go" | 1 first-in-class lead (ISM8969), cardiometabolic |
| TYK2 | data-rich, model most accurate → "already solved" | ~100 trials, drug approved (Sotyktu) |
| IRAK4 | no buildable+ownable design → "no" | 10 trials, pivotal study SUSPENDED, IP-saturated |

The two "noes" — ENPP1 (not yet) and IRAK4 (not here) — are the results that a purely optimistic pipeline would have buried. The clinical world independently confirms both were the right calls.
